Parts of Speech

Nouns

Nouns are words that represent a person, place, thing, or idea. Examples include:


  • John (person)
  • London (place)
  • Book (thing)
  • Love (idea)

Example :

  • The table is made of wood
  • She bought two dresses for the party
  • They are my big family

Pronouns

Pronouns are words that take the place of a noun. Examples include:


  • He (replaces a male noun)
  • She (replaces a female noun)
  • It (replaces a neutral noun)
  • They (replaces a plural noun)

Example :

  • I will study in Yogyakarta next month
  • You always do it well !
  • Did you talk nonesense about me ?

Verbs

Verbs are words that express an action, state, or occurrence. Examples include:


  • Run (action)
  • Be (state)
  • Happen (occurrence)

Example :

  • She runs every morning for exercise
  • They eat healthy food for their lunch
  • He Writes a new book every year

Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe or modify another person or thing in the sentence. Examples include:


  • Big (describes a noun)
  • Happy (describes a noun)
  • Red (describes a noun)

Example :

  • My Sister has curly hair
  • The sky is very pretty
  • My little sister doesn't like seblak because it's spicy

Adverbs

Adverbs are words that describe or modify a verb, adjective, or other adverb. Examples include:


  • Slowly (describes a verb)
  • Very (describes an adjective)
  • Extremely (describes an adverb)

Example :

  • She sings beatifully
  • H always wakes up early
  • They danced gracefully

Prepositions

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence.

Example :

  • Somebody is standing at the door
  • I put the book on the table
  • There is ice cream on the table
